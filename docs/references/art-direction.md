## 1. Visual Pillars

- **Premium indie sports feel** — broadcast-quality energy at a small, focused scope.
- **Stadium atmosphere** — the game should always feel like a live match under the lights.
- **Clarity first** — polish never comes at the cost of readability or focus on the goal and ball.
- **Cohesive identity** — menus and match share one visual language; menus are an extension of the stadium, not a separate world.

## 2. Mood & Atmosphere

- Vibrant, energetic, and confident; the emotional register is excitement and tension.
- Match-day lighting that flatters the pitch and keeps the goal and ball legible.
- A living crowd that swells and reacts, deepening immersion without distracting from play.
- Optional weather/atmospheric flavor for variety, only where it preserves clarity.

## 3. Key Elements (Direction Notes)

| Element | Direction |
|---|---|
| **Pitch** | Clean, attractive, unmistakably a football field; supports focus on the goal. |
| **Goal** | The visual anchor of every shot; outcomes must read instantly. |
| **Goalkeeper** | Expressive and readable; movement communicates anticipation and reaction. |
| **Ball** | Crisp and easy to track; the eye should follow it naturally. |
| **Stadium & crowd** | Convey scale, occasion, and energy; react to key moments. |
| **Menus & backgrounds** | On-brand, uncluttered, supportive of foreground content. |
| **Icons** | Simple, intuitive, immediately understandable. |
| **Trophies & achievements** | Aspirational and collectible; they should feel valuable. |

## 4. Consistency Checklist

- Shared spacing rhythm and alignment across all screens.
- Consistent interaction-state treatment (default, hover, selected, pressed, focused, disabled).
- Color is never the sole carrier of meaning (accessibility).
- Motion is smooth, subtle, meaningful, and non-distracting.

## 5. Open Questions / To Decide Later

- Final color palette and typography selections.
- Degree of stylization (e.g., flat vs. lightly rendered).
- Extent of weather/time-of-day variety in Version 1.
- Celebration intensity and frequency tuning.

*These notes should evolve alongside mockups and playtests.*
